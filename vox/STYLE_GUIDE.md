# Writing Style Guide

This applies to every piece of writing in this project: cover letters, resume bullets, application fields, LinkedIn copy, everything.

---

## PRE-OUTPUT CHECKLIST — Run before presenting any draft

- [ ] Search the output for the character "—". If found, rewrite that sentence. No exceptions.
- [ ] Cover letter: does any sentence read like a resume bullet? Rewrite it as narrative.
- [ ] Cover letter: are specific stats lifted directly from the resume (hours saved, headcount numbers, dashboard counts, gap counts)? Remove or reframe as story.
- [ ] Does any sentence contain a banned word from the AI vocabulary list? Rewrite it.

---

## Voice Rules

- First person, active voice throughout
- Direct and specific — no vague attributions ("helped drive," "contributed to")
- Honest about gaps — address them directly and briefly, then move on
- No overselling, no puffery
- Framing preference: honest representation of scope, not inflation

---

## Hard Prohibitions — Never Use

**Em dashes are banned entirely.** The character "—" must never appear in any output. Not for emphasis, not for asides, not for lists. Use a comma, a colon, or rewrite the sentence. This is the single most common error in this project. Check explicitly before presenting any draft.

**Other banned constructions:**
- Rule of three (listing three things for rhetorical effect)
- "Not X but Y" constructions
- "Overall" or "in conclusion"
- "Serves as" or "stands as"
- Superficial -ing phrases appended to sentences ("...driving impact," "...enabling growth")
- Passive voice

**AI vocabulary — banned words:**
delve, pivotal, tapestry, foster, testament, enhance, crucial, robust, garner, underscore, vibrant, meticulous, showcase, highlight (as verb), interplay, intricacies, align with, enduring, elevate, leverage (as verb), spearhead, synergy, impactful, transformative

**Closing patterns:**
- No formulaic "challenges/future" closings
- No "I look forward to the opportunity to..."
- Standard close: "Happy to connect if you want to dig into the specifics."

---

## Cover Letter Rules

Cover letters are not resume summaries. They tell the story behind the resume. Specific rules:

- Do not lift stats directly from the resume. No "150+ dashboards," "8 to 40 hours," "four structural gaps," "six VPs." Tell the narrative; let the resume carry the numbers.
- Each paragraph should read like a human wrote it in a letter, not like a bullet point with connective tissue added.
- The Meta paragraph must reflect the accurate narrative arc: You built this function, grew with it over many years, and led transformation from a position of deep institutional knowledge. You did not walk into a broken function someone else created. See VOX_CONTEXT.md for the full narrative.
- One gap acknowledgment maximum, brief, placed at the end of the body, confident not apologetic.

---

## Cover Letter Structure

1. **Opening hook** — specific to the company or role framing, not generic admiration. Pull a word or phrase from the JD that names the real problem.
2. **Primary narrative paragraph** — your career arc (see VOX_CONTEXT.md for the accurate narrative)
3. **Second employer paragraph** — key accomplishment from your most recent role
4. **Gap acknowledgment (if needed)** — one sentence, honest, confident, end of body
5. **Close** — "Happy to connect if you want to dig into the specifics."

---

## Resume Bullet Rules

- Lead with the action, not the context
- Quantify where real numbers exist — do not invent or round up
- "Defined requirements and managed delivery" not "Built" (Mike is a PM, not an IC engineer)
- [YOUR GAPS — do not imply experience you do not have]
- Financial modeling = workforce/demand/capacity planning only
- People leadership: [YOUR TEAM SIZE AND COMPOSITION]
